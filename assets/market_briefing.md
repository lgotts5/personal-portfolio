## Macro Read

**Equities: Tech is leading today** — Nasdaq (+1.02%) is outpacing the S&P 500 (+0.44%). The Nasdaq is heavy in tech and growth companies (Apple, Nvidia, Microsoft, Meta), so when it leads, it means investors are feeling confident and willing to buy riskier, high-growth names. This is called a *risk-on* environment.
The Dow (-0.15%) is red while Nasdaq is green — tech winning while traditional blue-chips (Boeing, Caterpillar, Goldman) lag. Classic growth-over-value setup.

**VIX (Fear Index) at 16.93** (normal range) — The VIX measures the market's expectation of S&P 500 volatility over the next 30 days, derived from options prices. Think of it as the market's 'anxiety meter.' It's up 0.04 points today. VIX between 15–20 is the market's 'calm but alert' zone. Options are fairly priced — no extreme fear or complacency. This is the baseline you'll see on most ordinary trading days. Rule of thumb: VIX < 20 = calm, 20–30 = worried, > 30 = fearful.

**Yield Curve: 3M at 3.575% vs. 10Y at 4.378% — spread: +0.803%** (normal (upward sloping)) — The yield curve plots interest rates across maturities. The 3M-10Y spread is the most-watched recession signal. The 10-year yield (4.378%) is notably higher than the 3-month (3.575%), which is the normal state — investors demand more return for locking up money longer. A steep curve is generally a sign of economic optimism and healthy credit conditions.

**Dollar Index (DXY) at 98.18** (weak, +0.03% today) — DXY measures the US dollar against a basket of major currencies (euro, yen, pound, etc.). It's the missing link that connects equities, commodities, and global markets. Dollar is up today. A weak dollar is a tailwind for commodities (gold, oil get cheaper to buy in other currencies → more demand), boosts earnings of US multinationals when foreign revenue is converted back, and attracts capital to emerging markets.

**10-Year Treasury at 4.378%** (moderate) — The risk-free baseline every asset is priced against. Rates aren't crushing growth, but aren't a strong tailwind either. Market is in 'wait and see' mode on the Fed. Today rates ticked down 1.2 bps — mild tailwind for stocks, especially tech.

**Gold at $4,634** (-0.11% today) — Gold historically traded $1,800–2,000. At $4,634 it reflects sustained macro anxiety. Watch gold vs. VIX — both elevated together = real fear.

**Oil (WTI) at $101.76** (-3.51% today) — Sharp drop. Lower oil reduces inflation pressure → less reason for the Fed to keep rates high. Bad for energy stocks (XOM, CVX), good for airlines and consumers.

**Copper ('Dr. Copper') at $5.966/lb** (-0.80% today, relatively flat today) — No strong growth signal from copper today — watch direction over the coming week. Also key for AI infrastructure: data centers and EV batteries require massive copper. Gold/Silver ratio: 60.7x (historical avg ~65x — ratio normal).

**FX Markets** —  EUR/USD at 1.1732 — stable. USD/JPY at 157.04 is elevated — BOJ intervention risk is real. Japan's central bank has previously stepped in to defend the yen near these levels. A sharp yen move would ripple through global carry trades. FX options priced using Garman-Kohlhagen (Black-Scholes with two risk-free rates). Forward rate: F = S·e^((r_d - r_f)·T) — rate differentials drive currency forwards.

**Credit Markets (proxy)** — LQD (-0.35%) and HYG (-0.52%) moving together — no stress signal from credit markets today. Credit spread = corporate yield − Treasury yield (same maturity). Widening spreads = rising default risk = risk-off signal. Altman Z-score: Z = 1.2X₁ + 1.4X₂ + 3.3X₃ + 0.6X₄ + 0.999X₅ — below 1.8 = distress zone.

**Bitcoin at $78,580** (+2.99% today) — Outperforming Nasdaq — crypto-specific buying (ETF flows, regulatory news, or 'digital gold' demand).

**Big picture today:** Clean risk-on — equities up, yields down, VIX calm. Ideal combo for growth stocks. No macro alarm bells.
